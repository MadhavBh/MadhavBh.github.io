    LITERATURE EVENTS

EXTEMPORE

Extemporaneous speaking requires the speaker to have a strong command of language, good research skills, and the ability to think on their feet, as they have to come up with a well-structured speech in a short period of time.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

Individual event

2 + 1 mins per participant

Topics given 5 minutes before event and participants prepare and present before the audience.

The individual will have to speak on an impromptu topic for a set amount of time.

Time: 1 hour

First 2 winners of prelims to be selected for finals on 11 to 13 April 2023.

QUIZ EVENTS

A quiz is a form of game or a mind sport in which players attempt to answer questions correctly on one or several specific topics. This intellectual rendezvous will test your wit and knowledge, and only the most incredible minds will rise above the pressure.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

3 members per team

Rounds to be decided by deanery.

Written prelims

Time: 1 hour

First 2 winning teams of the prelims to be selected for the finals on 11 to 13 April 2023

POT-POURRI

Here's a chance to showcase your knowledge and presentation skills as you, along with your teammates, navigate through Pot Pourri to the zenith of victory. It's the perfect culmination of various literary events, ranging from brain teasers to dumb charades and Pictionary.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

Time: 12+3 minutes (including setup and clearance time)

In case of failure to adhere to the allotted time limit, output volumes will be cut

Minimum of 4 and maximum of 8 members per team

Any Rhythmic accompaniment (Beat/Drum/Percussion) through a keyboard is not allowed.

A standard 5-piece Drum kit will be provided.

Growling is NOT allowed.

Bands MUST have at least one vocalist and not more than three vocalists.

Individual Prizes for Best Original Composition, Best Vocalist, Best Keyboardist, Best Guitarist, Best Bassist, Best Drummer.

DUMB CHARADES

Charades is a game of pantomimes: you have to "act out" a phrase or title without speaking, while the other members of your team try to guess what the phrase or title is. It facilitates awareness of how emotion, identity and social situations can be conveyed through faces.
